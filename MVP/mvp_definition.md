# MVP Definition 

UQ-Connect's Minimum Viable Product (MVP) is defined by 5 concrete features, each justified by evidence gathered across three iterations of user research, prototype testing, and hypothesis validation. 

---

## The 5 MVP Features 

### 1. Live Discovery Feed
A real-time feed of campus activities, showing activity name, location, skill tier, start time, and other information.

**Evidence:** In Iteration 1, 80% of students said they missed events due to information being scattered across Instagram, Discord, and email (Learning Card 1). In Iteration 3, tested users found an activity within 10 seconds on the Live Feed (Learning Card 2). This directly validated Hypothesis 2, confirming that immediacy drives spontaneous engagement.

**Therefore:** The Live Feed is set as the default tab of UQ-Connect.

---

### 2. Skill-Tiered Activities
Open social activities can carry a skill tier tag, such as Newbie, Hobbyist, or Competitive, which is displayed on activity cards and filterable by students.

**Evidence:** In Iteration 1, 80% of students stated they were more likely to join if they felt socially safe, emphasising their fear of "not being good enough" as a barrier (Learning Card 4).

**Therefore:** Skill Tier is a prominent field for social activities, and tier labels are colour-coded. 

---

### 3. Pathfinder Booking
A peer mentoring hub where students can browse senior mentor profiles and send a "Book Chat" request, supported by pre-written icebreaker prompts to help ease communication anxiety.

**Evidence:** In Iteration 2, over 70% of students said a 1-on-1 support feature would increase their confidence, but only 50% would book a chat, with some feedback describing it as feeling like a job interview (Learning Card 8). This refuted Hypothesis 3 and resulted in a change, renaming "Consultations" to "Pathfinder Chats" and replacing formal language with a more casual vibe and a simple "Book Chat" button. In Iteration 3, more than 60% of socially anxious students confirmed they would actually use the feature, but around 30% still hesitated due to not knowing what to say first (Learning Card 9), leading to the addition of pre-written icebreaker prompts.

**Therefore:** The feature is framed as a casual peer chat, not a formal booking, with icebreakers built into the chat window.

---

### 4. Clubs Directory
A searchable directory of the 220+ UQ clubs with category tabs like Academic/Non-Academic, subcategory filters (Sport, Art, Hobby, Tech), and club profiles displaying membership pricing and a Memories & Highlights section. 

**Evidence:** In Iteration 2, ~70% of students preferred filtering based on category as their main discovery method (Learning Card 6), and 90% wanted additional features such as past event highlights and activity indicators (Learning Card 7). In Iteration 3, 70% of students found at least one club they had never heard of while browsing with filters (Learning Card 12), and 85% siad that past memories increased their likelihood to join (Learning Card 4, MVP Hypotheses).

**Therefore:** Category filters are clearly displayed at the top of the directory, and club profiles have a Memories & Highlights field.

---

### 5. In-App Messaging 
A messaging system allowing students to chat with club & activity members and Pathfinder mentors in the app, reducing the reliance on other platforms for contact.

**Evidence:** In Iteration 1, more than 70% of students said they missed events and lost engagement because club communication was scattered across platforms like Instagram, Discord, and email (Learning Card 1). In Iteration 2, students wanted a way to ask questions and connect with members before committing to joining (Learning Card 7), with 90% suggesting features that would give them more information and connections before showing up. In Iteration 3, Learning Card 10 showed that fully replacing Discord and Messenger can be bothersome, meaning in-app messaging should complement rather than compete with these platforms, focusing on direct 1-on-1 and small group conversation. 

**Therefore:** In-app messaging is aimed at direct student-to-student and student-to-mentor conversations, with clubs keeping their Discord and Messenger links for broader communication.
