# 🧪 MVP Test Cards: Hypotheses & Validation

**Project:** UQ-Connect - MVP Validation  
**Version:** Iteration 2

---

## Hypothesis 1: The Skill-Gap Barrier

**What We Believed:**  
Students are 50% more likely to join an activity if they are guaranteed that everyone else is at their exact skill level (Beginner/Newbie).

**Test Design:**  
Conduct a "Painted Door" test using two mockups:
- **Mockup A:** Generic event title ("Tennis Social")
- **Mockup B:** Same event with "Beginner-Only" tier tag

Ask 10 socially anxious students which version they would be more likely to join.

**Success Metric:**  
We are right if at least 7 out of 10 socially anxious students choose the tiered version.

**What We're Testing:**  
Whether explicit skill transparency reduces the "impostor syndrome" barrier to participation.

---

## Hypothesis 2: Immediacy vs. Directory

**What We Believed:**  
Students prefer a "What's on Now" feed over a searchable directory of clubs.

**Test Design:**  
Show students two discovery methods:
- **Option A:** UQU Club Directory (searchable, category-based)
- **Option B:** Our "Live Feed" mockup (chronological, what's happening now)

Ask them: "Find an activity you'd want to do in the next hour."

**Success Metric:**  
We are right if the "Live Feed" is chosen as the preferred discovery method by 80% of participants.

**What We're Testing:**  
Whether time-sensitive discovery is more appealing than comprehensive but static directories.
