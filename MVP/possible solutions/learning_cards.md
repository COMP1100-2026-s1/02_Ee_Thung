# 🧠 MVP Learning Cards: User Research Insights

**Project:** UQ-Connect - MVP Definition  
**Version:** Iteration 2  
**Based on:** Thematic Analysis of Iteration 1 Interviews

---

## Learning Card 1: The "Social Safety" Validation

**What We Observed:**  
During interviews, participants (e.g., Keidi and Nathan) repeatedly mentioned being "scared" or "too shy" to join clubs where everyone else seemed like an expert or already knew each other.

**What We Learnt:**  
The "Social Safety" value proposition is not just about having a friend; it is about **skill-level transparency**. Students need to know they won't be the only inexperienced person in the room.

**Therefore We Will:**  
Prioritize the "Skill-Tier" tag as a **mandatory field** for every "Open Social" created in the MVP.

**Implication for MVP:**  
- Every Open Social must require a Newbie/Hobbyist/Competitive tier classification
- This is non-negotiable for addressing the core "social anxiety" barrier

---

## Learning Card 2: Information Decay

**What We Observed:**  
Ray and Nathan mentioned missing events because they didn't see the Instagram post in time or found the UQU site too static.

**What We Learnt:**  
Students don't need **more** information; they need **time-sensitive** information. A comprehensive club directory that never updates is less valuable than a live feed that shows what's available right now.

**Therefore We Will:**  
Focus the MVP UI on a **"Chronological Feed"** (Starting Now → Starting in 1 Hour) rather than a category-based search.

**Implication for MVP:**  
- Default view shows events ordered by start time (closest to now first)
- Category filtering is secondary to time-based discovery
- Events that have already started are removed from the feed

---

## Learning Card 3: The First-Step Barrier

**What We Observed:**  
Socially anxious students express hesitation not just about attending, but about making the initial contact or commitment to an activity.

**What We Learnt:**  
The first step matters most. Having a low-friction way to request human support (1-on-1 mentoring) can convert hesitant students into participants.

**Therefore We Will:**  
Make the "Request Pathfinder" button equally prominent as the "I'm Going" button.

**Implication for MVP:**  
- Pathfinder booking should be visible on every Open Social card
- This is a pathway for the most anxious segment to still engage
