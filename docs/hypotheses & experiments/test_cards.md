# 🧪 Master Test Cards: Hypotheses & Validation

**Project:** UQ-Connect (The Extracurricular Discovery Solution)  
**Document:** Consolidated Test Cards (Iterations 1, 2, and 3)

---

## 📅 Iteration 1: Problem Validation

### Hypothesis 1: Information Fragmentation
*   **We believe that:** Students feel overwhelmed by the sheer number of extracurricular options and struggle to find them because information is fragmented across too many platforms.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students to list the clubs they considered joining versus the clubs that they actually joined, so we can get a sense of selection and awareness of extracurriculars.
*   **And measure:** If 15 out of 20 interviewed students report only recognizing or joining top-tier or faculty-wide clubs, rather than niche hobbies.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if at least 12 out of 20 students (60%) explicitly state they feel choice paralysis and that the 220+ options made it harder to pick a club.

### Hypothesis 2: Discovery Channels (Market Day)
*   **We believe that:** Market Day is the primary way students discover clubs, and if they miss it, they remain disconnected for the rest of the semester.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Compare the extracurricular engagement levels of students who attended Market Day compared to those who did not.
*   **And measure:** The onboarding success rate among a sample of 30 students.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are correct if at least 24 out of 30 students (80%) who missed or felt overwhelmed by Market Day report that they don't know how to break into a club mid-semester.

### Hypothesis 3: Career vs. Interest Motivation
*   **We believe that:** International students prioritize clubs that provide social connection and belonging, whereas domestic students prioritize career-aligned activities.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask international and domestic students about their motivations for joining extracurriculars.
*   **And measure:** If at least 15 out of 20 international students mention words similar to "belonging", "home", and "connection", compared to 15 out of 20 domestic students mentioning "resume" or "skill-building".
*   **Success Criteria:** We're right if over 75% of international students rank social connection as their primary driver, while over 75% of domestic students prioritize career-aligned activities.

### Hypothesis 4: The "Social Safety" Factor
*   **We believe that:** Students are hesitant to join new activities because they are afraid of attending the first meeting alone without a pre-existing social connection.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students whether they would attend a meeting for their biggest interests if they had to go by themselves without any connections.
*   **And measure:** Ask a sample of 30 students if they would attend a meeting for their highest-interest club if no friends were going.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are correct if at least 23 out of 30 students (76%) who report high interest admit they would not attend a meeting if no one they knew was going.

## 📅 Iteration 2: Solution Validation

### Hypothesis 5: Fragmented Sign-Up Friction
*   **We believe that:** The current club discovery and sign-up process is fragmented, frustrating, and intimidating, creating barriers that prevent students from joining clubs and building community.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students about their current experience finding and signing up for clubs across online and in-person channels, and identify the specific friction points that prevent or discourage them from joining.
*   **And measure:** Categorize the types of frustration/intimidation factors students report (e.g., scattered information, unclear expectations, fear of commitment, social anxiety) and measure how frequently each barrier appears.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if more than 60% of students report at least one significant frustration or intimidating factor in their current club discovery process.

### Hypothesis 6: Browse and Filter Preferences
*   **We believe that:** Students have clear, converging preferences for how they prefer to browse and filter clubs (e.g., search bar, category tabs, degree-based filtering), and identifying these preferences will inform our MVP UX design.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students which navigation and filtering methods would feel most natural and intuitive when searching for relevant clubs on a centralized app or website.
*   **And measure:** Categorize student preferences across filtering methods (Search Bar, Category Tabs, Degree/Interest Filtering, Recommendation Feed, Quiz/Match) and measure which methods receive the highest preference.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if a clear majority (>60%) of students converge on one or two preferred filtering methods.

### Hypothesis 7: Unmet Feature Expectations
*   **We believe that:** Students have specific feature expectations and unmet needs when it comes to club discovery platforms — identifying these will reveal gaps in our MVP scope and opportunities for future enhancement.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students what additional features, information, or functionality they would want to see in a club discovery app to make them more likely to explore and join clubs.
*   **And measure:** Catalog all suggested features and measure the frequency of recurring themes (e.g., member reviews, event calendars, price transparency, event reminders).
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if more than 50% of students suggest at least one additional feature or information type not currently prioritized in our three-pillar solution.

### Hypothesis 8: One-on-One Support Bridge (Pivoted)
*   **We believe that:** One-on-one consultations with student ambassadors or club leaders would meaningfully reduce student hesitation and increase their confidence to join clubs, making it a critical feature for supporting our most vulnerable segments.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Present the concept of booking a casual, one-on-one chat with a student ambassador or club leader through the app, and measure whether students believe this would change their likelihood of joining clubs.
*   **And measure:** The percentage of students who say a 1-on-1 booking feature would increase their confidence, and whether they would realistically use it if available.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if more than 70% of students say they would find 1-on-1 meetings helpful in reducing hesitation, and at least 55% say they would realistically book one to learn more about a club before committing.

## 📅 Iteration 3: MVP & Migration Validation

### Hypothesis 9: Actual Pathfinder Engagement
*   **We believe that:** Reframing the Pathfinder feature as a casual peer booking will lead to actual usage (students sending messages when anxious) rather than being viewed as a purely hypothetical feature they would never use.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Present the interactive prototype's Pathfinder Hub to students and ask: *"After trying the Pathfinder feature in UQ Connect, did you feel like you'd actually send a message to a senior member, or did it feel more like something you might use one day but probably wouldn't?"*
*   **And measure:** The percentage of socially anxious students who indicate a clear, immediate intent to use it.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if at least 60% of anxious students express a clear, immediate intent to send a message when facing a real event they want to attend.

### Hypothesis 10: Platform Migration Hesitancy
*   **We believe that:** If a club decides to move all activity updates and chats to UQ Connect, students' primary source of hesitancy will be notification fatigue and the friction of checking a new app.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students: *"If your club's committee announced tomorrow that all activity updates and chats were moving to UQ Connect, what's the first thing that would make you hesitant?"*
*   **And measure:** The frequency of responses citing communication fragmentation or preference for existing platforms (like Discord/Facebook).
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if more than 70% of students express concern about notification reliability and the inconvenience of adding another daily communication channel.

### Hypothesis 11: Club Executive Workload & Adoption
*   **We believe that:** Club executives will perceive event creation on UQ-Connect as redundant work unless the app offers automated cross-posting to Facebook/Instagram/Discord.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Ask students who run or have run clubs: *"Imagine you're running a club and had to post every event through UQ Connect instead of Facebook, email, or other platforms. What would need to be true about the app for that to not feel like extra work?"*
*   **And measure:** The percentage of club execs who state that cross-posting capability or automated synchronization is essential for them to adopt the tool.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if at least 80% of club leaders state they would only adopt UQ Connect if it integrates with or cross-posts to their existing marketing channels.

### Hypothesis 12: Discovery vs. Familiarity
*   **We believe that:** The category-based filtering and search layout in UQ-Connect allows students to discover unfamiliar, niche clubs rather than just reinforcing awareness of top-tier clubs they already know.
*   **To verify this, we will:** Have students browse the Clubs Directory using filters (e.g., Hobby, Art, price range) and ask: *"When you were browsing clubs, did the search and filter help you find anything you didn't already know about, or were you mostly seeing clubs you'd heard of before?"*
*   **And measure:** The percentage of users who identify at least one new, unfamiliar club during their browsing session.
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if more than 65% of students discover a new, relevant club they were previously unaware of.

### Hypothesis 13: Activity Creation Flow Realism
*   **We believe that:** Club executives and hosts will find the activity creation and joining flow realistic for real campus events, but will identify critical missing operational features (like attendance caps and RSVP tracking).
*   **To verify this, we will:** Walk club execs through the "Host Open Social" creation flow in the prototype and ask: *"When you saw the activity creation and joining flow, did it feel like something a club exec would actually set up for a real event, or did it feel like it was missing things they'd need?"*
*   **And measure:** The frequency of suggestions regarding missing logistical fields (RSVP limits, equipment notes, ticket links).
*   **Success Criteria:** We are right if at least 70% of club executives find the layout realistic, but 100% request adding at least one operational field (like attendance caps) before deploying it in production.
