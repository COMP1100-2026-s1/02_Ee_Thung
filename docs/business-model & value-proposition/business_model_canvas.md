# 🗺️ Business Model Canvas
![alt text](<BMC_COMP1100 (1).png>)

## 👥 Customer Segments
- **Socially anxious domestic students** — local students who want to join extracurriculars but are daunted by the idea of attending alone
- **Connection-seeking international students** — students looking for a home away from home and cultural belonging
- **New UQ students** — students in their first few weeks who are most likely unfamiliar with the club system at UQ

## 💡 Value Propositions
- **Social Confidence** — reducing the emotional barrier to participation by making students feel safe and included, especially when attending events alone
- **Navigational Clarity** — solving the "Information Fragmentation" problem by saving students time and making it easier to choose from campus activities through a single, curated source of truth
- **Strategic Alignment** — helping students make the most of their university time by matching extracurricular activities with their career goals and personal well-being

## 🤝 Key Partners
- **UQ Union (UQU)** — the governing body for 220+ clubs; essential for data access, legitimacy, and official club integration
- **Club Executives** — individual club leaders who provide real-time updates on event "vibes" and schedules
- **UQ Life and UQ Sport** — partners for broader campus engagement, wellness initiatives, and facility access
- **Corporate & Faculty Sponsors** — professional bodies (e.g., EA, ACS) that validate the "Strategic Alignment" for career-focused students

## ⚙️ Key Activities
- **Platform Maintenance & Optimization** — ensuring the software remains a seamless, bug-free "source of truth"
- **Data Curation & Verification** — actively aggregating and updating fragmented club data into a consistent format
- **Community Engagement** — managing on-campus presence (Market Day) and fostering the "Social Safety" culture
- **Stakeholder Relationship Management** — maintaining trust and technical bridges with UQU and student societies

## 🛠️ Key Resources
- **Digital Infrastructure** — the proprietary platform, database, and recommendation algorithms
- **The "Student Ambassador" Network** — human capital used to drive the "Social Confidence" value proposition on the ground
- **Brand & Trust** — a reputation as the official, most reliable hub for student life information
- **Data Intellectual Property** — a structured, real-time map of UQ's extracurricular ecosystem

## 💬 Customer Relationships
- **Personalized Automation** — algorithmic matching of activities to a student's career path and wellness profile
- **Peer-to-Peer Community** — a "Social Safety" layer where students can find "buddies" or check in with club seniors
- **Self-Service** — a high-efficiency, low-touch digital interface for quick discovery
- **On-the-Ground Support** — direct human interaction during high-anxiety periods like O-Week and Market Day

## 📣 Channels
- **Market Day** — the primary high-traffic touchpoint in the Great Court; workers advertising the product to new students
- **Digital Discovery Platforms** — UQ Life website, Discord servers, and Instagram "Club Spotlights"
- **Lecture/Tutorial Shilling** — direct announcements in high-density courses (e.g., CSSE2010 or MATH2001) to lower the "discovery" barrier

## 💸 Cost Structure
- **Platform R&D and Hosting** — ongoing costs for software development, AWS/Cloud hosting, and security
- **Marketing & Acquisition** — costs associated with Market Day stalls, physical collateral, and student ambassador stipends
- **Operations & Events** — direct costs for running high-value mixers or professional networking nights
- **Customer Support & Moderation** — maintaining the safety and accuracy of the community and data feed

## 💰 Revenue Streams
- **Membership Fees** — standard $2–$10 entry fees for club access and insurance
- **Partnerships** — partnerships with the likes of UQ Union who want to improve the accessibility of extracurriculars
