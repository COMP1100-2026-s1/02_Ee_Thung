# Value Proposition Canvas: UQ Extracurricular Engagement

---

## 1. Segment: Socially Anxious Domestic Students
*Local students who want to join but are daunted by the idea of attending alone.*

### **Customer Profile (The Student)**
* **Gains:** Resume enhancement, finding a "wingman," breaking study monotony.
* **Pains:** High "social cost" of entry, "The Ghost" syndrome (attending but staying silent).
* **Customer Jobs:** Balancing high-pressure courses (e.g., MATH2001, CSSE2010) with a low-stakes social life.

### **Value Map (The Solution)**
* **Gain Creators:** "First-timer" mixers, peer-mentoring, and "no-friend-needed" event tags.
* **Pain Relievers:** Mandatory "Icebreaker" protocols and a **Buddy System** to act as a social anchor.
* **Products & Services:** A "Socially Safe" club accreditation that guarantees structured onboarding for solo attendees.

**Hypothesis Status:**
* **ACCEPTED:** *The "Friend" Requirement.* Data confirms >75% of this segment won't attend a first meeting alone.
* **ACCEPTED:** *Clique Factor.* Seniors knowing each other acts as a primary deterrent for domestic newcomers.


---


## 2. Segment: Connection-Seeking International Students
*Students looking for a home away from home and cultural belonging.*

### **Customer Profile (The Student)**
* **Gains:** Cultural familiarity, learning local customs/slang, exploring Queensland (e.g., Hiking Club).
* **Pains:** Social isolation, missing the Market Day "window," overwhelming choice (220+ clubs).
* **Customer Jobs:** Building a reliable support network and social "safety net" from scratch.

### **Value Map (The Solution)**
* **Gain Creators:** Cultural exchange nights and "Exploration" trips designed for those new to Australia.
* **Pain Relievers:** Language-diverse onboarding materials and simplified "interest-based" club filtering.
* **Products & Services:** "The Global Nest"—a concierge service for international student club placement.

**Hypothesis Status:**
* **ACCEPTED:** *The Connection Driver.* Motivation is primarily "Belonging" over "Hobby."
* **REFUTED (Partial):** *The Market Day Bottleneck.* While they may miss the physical event, they are actually **more** likely to proactively seek clubs mid-semester than domestic students due to higher social necessity.


---


## 3. Segment: New UQ Students (O-Week / Market Day)
*Students in their first few weeks who are unfamiliar with the club system.*

### **Customer Profile (The Student)**
* **Gains:** Free food/merch, "Market Day" excitement, finding niche hobbies (e.g., Anime or Potter societies).
* **Pains:** Choice paralysis (220+ options), physical intimidation of crowds, feeling "behind" if Week 1 is missed.
* **Customer Jobs:** Navigating a massive campus and understanding UQ's complex club hierarchy.

### **Value Map (The Solution)**
* **Gain Creators:** Curated "Club Starter Packs" (e.g., The "First Year Engineering" or "Creative Arts" Pack).
* **Pain Relievers:** A digital "Club Match Quiz" to filter the 220+ options into a top 3. Mid-semester "Second Chance" sign-up days.
* **Products & Services:** "The UQ Navigator"—a streamlined discovery app that bypasses the Market Day chaos.

**Hypothesis Status:**
* **ACCEPTED:** *Choice Paralysis.* Students default to "Big Names" (UQBC, EUS) because the minor 200+ are too hard to filter.
* **ACCEPTED:** *The Bottleneck.* Missing Market Day frequently results in zero club memberships for the entire academic year.
