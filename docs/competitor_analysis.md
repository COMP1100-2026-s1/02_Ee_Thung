# Competitor Analysis

| | **Competitor** | **Target Market** | **Key Value Propositions** | **Weaknesses** | **Notes** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **UQ Union Website** | UQ students seeking to browse and register for clubs | Official, comprehensive directory of all UQ-affiliated clubs and societies; directly integrated with club registration and membership payment | Outdated UI; difficult to navigate; no way to gauge club activity or culture; purely administrative, no community or discovery features | The most "official" solution but functions more as a database than an engaging platform |
| 2 | **Rubric** | UQ students managing academic and campus life | All-in-one UQ student app; integrates timetables, grades, and some campus information in a familiar mobile format | Club discovery is not a core feature; very limited social or community functionality; not designed around interest-based exploration | Already installed by many UQ students, giving it a head start in adoption, but clubs are not its focus |
| 3 | **Facebook Groups** | Broad; used by university clubs globally for community building | Free; widely used and familiar; supports group chats, event listings, photo sharing, and announcements in one place | Declining use among younger students; requires a Facebook account; no UQ-specific filtering or discovery; not designed for club sign-up | Many UQ clubs use Facebook Groups as their primary communication channel, making it a de facto tool rather than a deliberate choice |
| 4 | **Eventbrite** | General public and students looking for local events | Clean event discovery interface; filtering by date, category, and location; easy registration and ticketing flow | Not university-specific; no ongoing club membership or community features; focused on one-off events rather than sustained engagement | Some UQ clubs use Eventbrite to manage event sign-ups, but it plays no role in helping students find or join clubs long-term |
| 5 | **Meetup.com** | Adults seeking interest-based local communities | Strong interest-based filtering and group discovery; built around recurring events and ongoing community; accessible to newcomers | Not student or university-specific; skews toward older demographics; paid model for organisers limits uptake from volunteer-run student clubs | Closest in concept to the proposed app in terms of interest-based discovery, but lacks any UQ context or student-oriented design |
| 6 | **Our Product** *(proposed)* | UQ students, particularly those new to campus or hesitant to engage with clubs | UQ-specific club discovery with interest and degree-based filtering; member-written culture descriptions; ambassador chat feature; low-commitment "I'm interested" interactions; event reminders for saved clubs | Currently in early design phase; no existing user base; relies on clubs actively maintaining their profiles to stay useful | Designed specifically to address the gap between passive interest and actual club membership, with a focus on reducing social intimidation |
