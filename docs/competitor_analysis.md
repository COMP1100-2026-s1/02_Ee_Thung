# Competitor Analysis

| | **Competitor** | **Target Market** | **Key Value Propositions** | **Weaknesses** | **Notes** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **UQ Union Website** | UQ students browsing and registering for clubs | Official directory of all 220+ UQ clubs with integrated membership registration and payment | No live activity feed, purely administrative with no culture or skill-level context, as well as no mentoring or discovery features | Closest to our Clubs Directory but functions as a static database with no engagement layer |
| 2 | **Rubric** | UQ students managing academic and campus life | All-in-one UQ student app with timetable and grade integration and a familiar mobile format | No live event feed, no skill-tiered activities, no mentoring feature, and clubs are not a focus | High existing adoption among UQ students but does not address social anxiety or activity discovery |
| 3 | **Facebook Groups** | University clubs seeking free community tools | Free and familiar, supports event listings, group chats, announcements, and photo sharing | No skill-tier filtering, no live feed sorted by time, no structured mentoring and declining use among younger students | Used by many UQ clubs as default communication, but students must already know the club exists to find it |
| 4 | **Eventbrite** | General public and students looking for one-off events | Clean event discovery, easy registration and ticketing. Date and category filters | No skill-level tags, no ongoing club community, no mentoring, and not UQ-specific | Some clubs use it for ticketed events but importantly plays no role in helping students discover clubs or reduce social hesitation |
| 5 | **Meetup.com** | Adults seeking interest-based local communities | Interest-based group discovery built around recurring events and newcomer-friendly groups | No skill-tier matching, no university context, no mentoring feature, and skews older. Paid model limits student club uptake | Conceptually closest to our live feed and open socials model but completely lacks any UQ-specific design or student focus |
| 6 | **Our Product, UQ Connect** | UQ students, particularly those new to campus or hesitant to engage with clubs | Live activity feed showing what is happening now, skill-tiered open socials removing fear of inadequacy, one-on-one Pathfinder booking with senior mentors, searchable Clubs Directory with membership info and social proof | Early stage with no existing user base, relying on clubs actively maintaining profiles and posting activities to stay useful | The only solution addressing all four core barriers: information being fragmented, social anxiety, isolation, and decision-making friction |
